## Public Opinion Prediction: Wuhan University Case Study

<Info>
**Real Example**: MiroFish successfully predicted public opinion evolution for the Wuhan University dormitory formaldehyde incident using BettaFish-generated reports.
</Info>

### Scenario Setup

**Seed Material**: Public opinion report on university dormitory safety concerns  
**Simulation Duration**: 72 simulated hours  
**Agent Count**: 100+ individuals representing students, faculty, media, and officials  
**Platforms**: Twitter and Reddit parallel simulation

### Prediction Process

<Steps>
  <Step title="Graph Construction">
    MiroFish extracted entities (students, university, media outlets) and relationships from the seed report, building a knowledge graph using Zep Cloud's GraphRAG technology.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Agent Generation">
    Each entity was transformed into a detailed agent with:
    - Unique personality (MBTI type, age, profession)
    - Memory of the incident context
    - Behavioral patterns and communication style
  </Step>
  <Step title="Parallel Simulation">
    Agents interacted freely on both platforms:
    - Creating posts expressing opinions
    - Replying to and amplifying others' voices
    - Following and muting based on alignment
  </Step>
  <Step title="Report Generation">
    The Report Agent analyzed simulation results using multiple retrieval tools to produce a comprehensive prediction report with agent quotes and trend analysis.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Key Insights Predicted

**Platform-Specific Behavior**:
- Twitter agents generated 68% of initial outbreak volume with emotional, viral content
- Reddit discussions were more analytical, focusing on institutional accountability

**Sentiment Evolution**:
- Initial 24 hours: Anger and concern dominated (78% negative sentiment)
- 48-72 hours: Demand for transparency and concrete action increased

**Influential Voices**:
- Student agents became opinion leaders through personal testimonials
- Official responses that felt bureaucratic were criticized heavily

> "The university's response pattern appeared rigid and slow in the fast-paced social media environment." — Predicted agent sentiment from simulation

## Creative Writing: Dream of the Red Chamber

<Info>
**Real Example**: MiroFish predicted the lost ending of the classic Chinese novel *Dream of the Red Chamber* based on the first 80 chapters (hundreds of thousands of words).
</Info>

### How It Works for Fiction

Unlike traditional plot prediction models, MiroFish treats characters as independent agents with:
- **Rich backstories** extracted from existing text
- **Relationship dynamics** mapped as a knowledge graph
- **Personality traits** that drive authentic interactions
- **Memory** of past events that influence future behavior

### Simulation Setup

**Seed Material**: First 80 chapters of Dream of the Red Chamber  
**Characters Simulated**: 50+ main characters (Jia Baoyu, Lin Daiyu, Xue Baochai, etc.)  
**Interaction Environment**: Custom social platform modeling the Jia household  
**Simulation Rounds**: 200+ rounds tracking character development

## Financial Forecasting Scenarios

<Warning>
MiroFish is a research tool. Financial predictions should be validated with professional analysis and should not be the sole basis for investment decisions.
</Warning>

### Market Reaction Simulation

**Use Case**: Predict how different investor personas react to policy changes, earnings announcements, or market shocks.

**Setup Process**:
1. **Define Investor Personas**: Create agents representing retail investors, institutional traders, analysts, and influencers
2. **Inject Market Event**: Upload news, financial reports, or policy documents as seed material
3. **Run Simulation**: Observe buy/sell behaviors, sentiment shifts, and information cascades
4. **Analyze Patterns**: Report Agent identifies key price drivers and sentiment trends

**Example Scenarios**:
- Central bank interest rate decision impact
- Tech company earnings surprise reactions
- Regulatory announcement effects on sector sentiment

### Agent Capabilities

<Accordion title="Retail Investor Agents">
  - React emotionally to news and social sentiment
  - Follow influencers and trending opinions
  - Make decisions based on fear/greed cycles
  - Share opinions on social platforms
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Institutional Trader Agents">
  - Analyze fundamentals more carefully
  - React to peer institutional moves
  - Consider regulatory implications
  - Communicate through official channels
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Analyst & Media Agents">
  - Publish analysis and opinions
  - Influence both retail and institutional sentiment
  - Amplify or dampen market narratives
</Accordion>

## Policy Testing and Analysis

### Crisis Simulation

**Objective**: Test government or organizational crisis response strategies before real implementation.

**Example: Public Health Policy**

<Steps>
  <Step title="Baseline Population">
    Create agents representing different demographics, professions, and political leanings with realistic social networks.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Crisis Injection">
    Introduce a simulated crisis event (e.g., disease outbreak, safety incident) through seed documents.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Policy Intervention">
    Test different communication strategies, transparency levels, and action timelines.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Outcome Analysis">
    Measure public compliance, trust levels, misinformation spread, and sentiment evolution.
  </Step>
</Steps>

**Metrics MiroFish Tracks**:
- Sentiment distribution over time
- Information propagation patterns
- Influential agent identification
- Compliance vs. resistance ratios
- Trust in official sources

### Market Research Applications

**Product Launch Predictions**:
- Simulate consumer reactions to product announcements
- Identify potential PR risks before launch
- Optimize messaging for different audience segments

**Brand Crisis Management**:
- Test different apology strategies
- Predict boycott likelihood and scale
- Identify effective mitigation approaches

## Getting Started with Your Use Case

<Steps>
  <Step title="Prepare Seed Material">
    Gather relevant documents, reports, or data about your scenario (PDF, Markdown, or TXT format, up to 50MB).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Define Prediction Requirements">
    Clearly articulate what you want to predict in natural language (e.g., "How will students react to the new campus policy over the next 3 days?").
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure Simulation">
    Set simulation duration (in simulated hours), choose platforms (Twitter/Reddit/Both), and optionally limit rounds for testing.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Run and Analyze">
    Start the simulation and monitor real-time agent activities. Once complete, review the auto-generated prediction report.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
**Recommended Starting Point**: Run a small simulation (under 40 rounds) first to understand API consumption and validate your seed material quality before scaling up.
</Note>
